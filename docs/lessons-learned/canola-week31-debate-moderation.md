### 2. The Flow Arithmetic (Where Step 3.5 Goes Wrong)

Step 3.5 Flash sees:
- Exports YTD: 4,484.3 Kt (-28% YoY) ← **True**
- Deliveries YTD: 12,044.7 Kt (153% of 5yr avg) ← **True**
- Conclusion: "Supply is flooding the system" ← **False**

The error is treating YTD exports as a FLOW indicator when it's actually a CUMULATIVE position. The real question is: **Is the system absorbing supply RIGHT NOW?**

Week 31 evidence:
- Deliveries IN: ~455.6 Kt (CW, including Process)
- Stocks CHANGE: -175.6 Kt (draw, not build)

**If 631 Kt is leaving the system in a single week while 455 Kt enters, the system is in net-draw mode.** This is the opposite of "failing to absorb." The -28% YoY export gap is being partially covered by strong crush (6,274.9 Kt CY, only -2% YoY) and the math works: crush + exports + domestic use > deliveries = stocks decline.

## The Moderation Ruling

### Step 3.5 Flash made three specific errors:

**Error 1: Conflating YTD position with current flow**
- YTD exports are -28% YoY. True. But the WEEKLY flow shows stocks drawing, meaning current-week absorption > current-week supply. Step 3.5 anchored on the cumulative stat and missed the inflection.

**Error 2: Ignoring the stock draw as a signal**
- Step 3.5 flagged the -229.8 Kt stock draw as a "watch" item needing "2-3 more weeks to confirm." But the data shows draws in BOTH Wk 29 (-50.2) and Wk 31 (-175.6). That's two out of three recent weeks with net outflows. The confirmation Step 3.5 was waiting for already happened.

**Error 3: Missing the logistics constraint explanation**
- Step 3.5 concluded "weak exports = bearish." But weak exports + port congestion + strong crush + declining stocks = the system is absorbing supply through domestic channels while waiting for port capacity to clear. This is a bullish-with-timing-risk story, not a bearish one.

### What Grok got right:

- "Stock drawdown shows demand bite despite export lag" — Correct. The draw IS the demand.
- "Target basis strength" — Correct. When stocks draw at country level, basis narrows.
- "Hold patient" — Correct directional advice. The logistics bottleneck will clear, and when it does, both exports AND basis improve.

### What Grok should sharpen:

- Be specific about WHEN: "Vancouver vessel queue is 26 (avg 20) with heavy precip forecast. Once queue clears in 2-3 weeks, export pace accelerates and validates the bullish case."
- Flag the China tariff timing: "March 1 tariff reduction to 15% opens a new channel. Watch whether Chinese buying shows up in CGC Week 32-34 data."

## Agent Improvement Takeaways

### For Step 3.5 Flash (analyze-market-data):

1. **RULE: Stock direction trumps YTD position.** If stocks are drawing while deliveries are high, the system IS absorbing supply. Never call this bearish. The correct read is: "Demand is strong enough to draw inventory despite heavy farmer selling."

2. **RULE: Always compute the implied weekly absorption.** `Weekly Absorption = CW Deliveries + |WoW Stock Draw|`. If absorption exceeds deliveries, the system is in net-draw mode. This is structurally bullish regardless of where YTD exports sit.

3. **RULE: When export lag coexists with stock draw, look for a logistics constraint.** The default explanation is NOT "weak demand." It's "the pipe is full." Check port capacity, vessel queues, and out-of-car time before concluding demand is weak.

4. **RULE: Don't wait for 2-3 weeks of confirmation when the data already shows 2 out of 3 weeks drawing.** That IS the confirmation.
