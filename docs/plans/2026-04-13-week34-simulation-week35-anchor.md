- general idea: resilient exports, but softening futures and caution for grain still in bins

### Canola
Week 34 thesis on board:
- stance_score: +40
- general idea: crush support and better China backdrop offset weak exports

What held up:
- Crush still did its job. YTD crush is +1.0% YoY and commercial stocks still drew 18.7 tonnes WoW in Week 35.
- Canola held above 700 CAD/tonne.

What did not hold up well enough:
- Export lag is still serious at -22.1% YoY.
- Price confirmation is not strong enough to justify a confident +40 read.
- The spec long crowding risk remains real.

What to change in the thesis:
- Tone down the bullish conviction.
- Bull case should lead with domestic crush and stock draw.
- Bear case should clearly say the export program is still behind and the outside oilseed complex can pull this lower fast.

Week 35 anchor lean:
- Score: +10 to +20
- Recommendation: PATIENCE or WATCH
- Farmer read: canola still has support under it, but this is not a clean hold-everything bull. Stay patient, but be ready to price rallies.

## Bull/bear writing changes needed

Bushel Board should tighten the weekly thesis writing in these ways:

1. Lead with logistics for short-term calls
- The first paragraph should say what the pipeline did this week.
- Exports, receipts, processing, stocks, and farmer movement come first.

2. Remove weak generic macro filler
- Avoid broad statements like tariffs, energy costs, or weather unless they clearly moved this week’s call.
- If the signal did not change the recommendation this week, it belongs lower in the thesis.

3. Use the farmer summary framing more literally
- bull_case = what is helping the farmer this week
- bear_case = what is hurting the farmer this week
- final assessment = why that balance means hold it, watch it, or haul it

4. Add stronger score discipline
- Canola and barley should not get +40 to +55 unless price and logistics both confirm.
- Soybeans must apply the empty-pipeline trap before any bullish read.
